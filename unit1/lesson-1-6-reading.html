The Smith System: 5 Keys to Defensive Driving

The Smith System was developed in 1952 and has trained millions of professional drivers. These five principles, combined with IPDE, create a comprehensive defensive driving approach.

1

Aim High in Steering

Look 12–15 seconds down the road — about 3 blocks at 45 mph. Early warning of hazards, smoother steering, better lane position.

2

Get the Big Picture

See the entire environment — scan intersections side to side, check mirrors, maintain 360° awareness. The more you see, the more time you have.

3

Keep Your Eyes Moving

Shift gaze every 2 seconds — road, mirrors, sides, back. Prevents fixation, maintains awareness, reduces fatigue. The Identify phase in constant motion.

4

Leave Yourself an Out

Maintain a space cushion with escape routes. Never box yourself in. Always have somewhere to go if things go wrong. The Decide phase — always having options.

5

Make Sure They See You

Signal early, make eye contact at intersections, use headlights in low visibility, position your vehicle where others can see it. Being visible prevents crashes.

💡 Did You Know?

UPS and FedEx train all drivers on the Smith System. UPS drivers look 15 seconds ahead, contributing to one of the lowest crash rates in the industry — over 10 billion miles driven safely.

The Space Cushion

Your space cushion is open space around your vehicle in all directions.

  • Front: 3-second following distance (4+ behind trucks or bad weather)
  • Rear: If tailgated, increase front cushion — gradual stops give tailgaters more time
  • Sides: Stay centered. Stagger position — don't drive alongside other vehicles
  • Escape routes: Always know where you'd swerve. Keep one open lane or shoulder
✓ Pro Tip

Your space cushion is dynamic — it grows with speed, bad weather, heavy traffic, and proximity to hazards.

Commentary Driving

Commentary driving is narrating observations out loud while driving. It trains your brain to run IPDE and the Smith System automatically.

"Approaching intersection… stale green… scanning left, clear… right, pickup slowing… pedestrian on corner, covering brake… clear through… rearview — safe distance… school zone ahead, slowing…"
✓ Start as a Passenger

Try commentary driving silently in your head next time you're riding. Call out hazards, predictions, decisions. Best way to build habits before you drive.

Managing the Unexpected

  1. Don't panic. Slamming brakes or jerking the wheel can make things worse
  2. Assess quickly with IPDE: What is it? What will happen? Options?
  3. Execute smoothly. Controlled inputs — abrupt actions cause loss of control
  4. Use your space cushion. This is why you maintain escape routes

Common situations:

  • Car pulls out: Brake progressively, steer to open space
  • Animal in road: Brake firmly, do NOT swerve into oncoming traffic
  • Tire blowout: Grip wheel, ease off gas, steer straight, pull over slowly
  • Sudden stop ahead: Your 3-second cushion gives time. Brake, check mirrors, steer around if safe

📘 Key Vocabulary

Smith System
Five principles: Aim High, Big Picture, Eyes Moving, Leave an Out, Make Sure They See You.
Space Cushion
Open space around your vehicle in all directions — front, rear, sides, escape routes.
Commentary Driving
Narrating observations and decisions out loud to build IPDE and Smith System habits.
Target Fixation
Steering toward what you stare at. Overcome by keeping eyes moving and looking where you WANT to go.
Escape Route
Pre-identified path to avoid a collision — open lane, shoulder, or space for emergency steering.
Staggered Position
Not driving directly alongside another vehicle — maintaining side space and visibility.
