LESSON 1.6 KNOWLEDGE CHECK — Putting It All Together
=====================================================
5 Questions | 1 point each | 3 attempts

Q1: Which Smith System principle tells you to look 12-15 seconds down the road?
A) Get the Big Picture
B) Keep Your Eyes Moving
C) Aim High in Steering  ← CORRECT
D) Leave Yourself an Out
FEEDBACK: "Aim High in Steering" means looking far ahead — about 12-15 seconds of travel time, or 3 blocks at 45 mph. This gives early warning and smoother steering.

Q2: "Leave Yourself an Out" means:
A) Always have an exit strategy when parking
B) Maintain a space cushion with escape routes so you always have somewhere to go  ← CORRECT
C) Keep your windows open
D) Always drive in the right lane
FEEDBACK: This principle is about maintaining options. Your space cushion provides escape routes — open lanes, shoulders, or space to steer into if something goes wrong.

Q3: What is commentary driving?
A) Listening to a driving instructor's commentary
B) Reading road signs out loud
C) Narrating your driving observations and decisions out loud to build awareness habits  ← CORRECT
D) Talking to passengers about traffic
FEEDBACK: Commentary driving trains your brain to process IPDE and the Smith System automatically by verbalizing what you see, predict, and decide in real time.

Q4: When a car is tailgating you, the safest response is to:
A) Brake hard to teach them a lesson
B) Speed up to create distance
C) Increase your front following distance so you can stop more gradually  ← CORRECT
D) Pull over immediately
FEEDBACK: Increasing your front cushion compensates for the lost rear cushion. If you need to stop, a gradual stop gives the tailgater behind you more time to react.

Q5: Which of the following best describes the "space cushion" concept?
A) The air between your car and the road
B) Open space maintained around your vehicle in all directions — front, rear, sides, and escape routes  ← CORRECT
C) The distance between parked cars
D) The gap between your car and the speed limit
FEEDBACK: The space cushion is your protective zone in all directions. It's dynamic — it grows with speed, bad weather, and proximity to hazards like trucks and intersections.
